About DiscoverLanka

Built to Help Travelers Explore Sri Lanka Better

DiscoverLanka is a university student project currently in development, created to make Sri Lanka travel discovery and trip planning easier, clearer, and more inspiring.

Our Project Story

DiscoverLanka is designed as a travel-focused website concept that introduces destinations, supports trip planning, and helps travelers explore Sri Lanka in a more organized way.

Who We Are

DiscoverLanka is being developed as a student academic project with the goal of combining web design, user experience, and travel planning into a practical digital platform.

The website is not yet a full commercial travel service. It is currently in its development phase and is being improved step by step as part of learning, design, and system development work.

What We Want to Build

The goal is to create a platform where users can discover Sri Lankan destinations, explore travel categories, read traveler feedback, and use planning tools to shape their own trip more easily.

The long-term vision is a cleaner and smarter travel experience focused on Sri Lanka.

What DiscoverLanka Offers

The project is being developed around several key travel-focused features

Destination Discovery

Browse beaches, hill country, heritage sites, wildlife destinations, and more.

Trip Planning Ideas

Use structured planning pages to think through routes, stops, and itinerary flow.

Printable Itinerary Concepts

Support travelers with organized trip information that can be easier to review and print.

Traveler Feedback

Collect and display reviews and user experiences in a more engaging way.

Important Project Note

This website is still being improved and expanded

Development Phase

DiscoverLanka is currently a student project and remains under active development. Some features are conceptual, some sections are still improving, and certain information may be simplified for academic and demonstration purposes.

The website is intended to showcase design, structure, and travel planning ideas rather than act as a finalized commercial tourism platform.
